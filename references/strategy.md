# Maps, roles, and timings

## Map archetypes

### Open land maps (Arabia, Runestones, Serengeti)
- Aggression starts in Feudal; walling is possible but contested.
- Standard openings: Scouts (20-21 pop), pre-mill Drush, Drush→FC, Archers/Men-at-Arms (21-23 pop). Feudal pressure into Castle Age Knights or Crossbows.
- Eco is exposed: value mobility, map control, and forward towers.

### Closed maps (Black Forest, Amazon Tunnel, Hideout)
- Narrow chokes; both sides usually wall fully and boom.
- Plan for the long game: 3 TC boom → Imperial deathball. Fast Castle is standard;
  Feudal rushing rarely pays off through a walled choke.
- Trade is enormous in team games — protect the trade line, add markets early Imp.
- Winning comps: siege (Siege Onager/Bombard/Trebs) + ranged mass + meatshield, or
  raw-power units (Elephants, Paladins) that fight in chokes where mobility matters less.
- Siege Onagers cut alternate paths through the forest — both a threat and a tool.
- Petards/Trebs to break stone-wall chokes; control relics (often mid-map).

### Hybrid/water (Four Lakes, Baltic, Islands, Nomad)
- Fish boom is the strongest eco in the game; whoever controls water often out-booms.
- Islands: Galley-line war decides everything; Fire Ships counter Galleys up close,
  Galleys outrange Fires in mass. Check dock techs (Shipwright, Dry Dock) in civ file.
- Nomad: no starting TC; boar/dock start, watch for TC-deny aggression and early docks.

### Arena (stone-walled start)
- Safe eco until Castle Age. Common plans: Fast Castle → Boom, FC → Unique Unit or
  Knights all-in, Monk+Siege push, or sneak Villagers/rams. Fast Imp with strong
  imp powerspikes (e.g., Turks) is viable. Relic control matters (Lithuanians!).

## Team game roles

### Flank (outside seat, faces an enemy directly)
- Fights early: Archers or Scouts in Feudal, applies pressure, walls own eco.
- Archer civs shine here (range = defends and attacks across a wide front).

### Pocket (protected middle seat)
- Boom-oriented: usually Fast Castle into Knights (16-18 min), then either help a
  flank fight or out-boom everyone.
- Cavalry civs shine: Knights arrive fast across the map; camels if enemy pocket is
  also cavalry.
- On closed maps the pocket becomes the trade/eco engine and late-game power civ
  (Elephants, Paladins, strong siege).
- Pocket responsibilities: deliver timely Knight support, control mid resources/relics,
  set up trade in team games, tech switch to counter the enemy deathball.

## Timing benchmarks (decent club level, adjust ±)
- Feudal: 21 pop ≈ 9:40-10:30 (scouts), 22-23 pop ≈ 10:30-11:30 (archers/MAA)
- Castle: standard ≈ 16-18 min; Fast Castle 27-28 pop ≈ 15:30-16:30
- Imperial: boom ≈ 28-34 min; Fast Imp (closed maps) ≈ 24-27 min
- 3 TC boom: aim ~120 villagers+trade by mid-Imperial on closed maps; on open maps
  60-80 vils and fight.

## Answering "what's my best unit?"
Weigh, in order:
1. **Civ power unit** — what the civ file says is fully upgraded + boosted by bonuses/UTs.
2. **Map** — closed maps favor slow power units + siege; open maps favor mobility.
3. **Role** — pocket = cavalry/boom bias; flank = ranged bias.
4. **Game phase** — Feudal/Castle answer differs from the Imperial deathball.
5. **Gold** — long closed-map games end in trash wars unless trade holds; note the
   civ's trash quality as the fallback.
6. **Enemy comp** (if known) — apply counters.md, verified against the civ file.

## Worked example
"Persians pocket on Black Forest": Castle Age = Knights off a fast boom (Persian TC
bonus makes the boom stronger). Imperial = War Elephants + trade as the wincon in
chokes, with Savar as the mobile/anti-ranged arm (team bonus: +2 vs ranged), Heavy
Camels if the enemy commits to cavalry too. Screen elephants against Monks (Persians
lack Heresy — elephants are convertible; bring Hussars to snipe Monks) and against
Halberdiers (mix in your own ranged/siege; Persians have Bombard Cannon + Siege Ram
but no Siege Onager).
